Introduction

People increased their ability in terms of sensibility, empathy, speculation, etc. through aesthetic engagement in daily life design, art, and philosophy. With the maturation of ubiquitous digital development, we also see the commercial success led by aesthetic differentiate. The HCI community could contribute to aesthetics more. However, there are still several issues remained in practicing aesthetics on user interface and interaction in terms of the epistemological position, human factors, and quantification. The aim of this workshop is to increase the momentum for aesthetic research within HCI and related field. For this, we plan to create a space for discussing and promoting the consensus towards further computational aesthetics. We thus welcome contributions to theories, methodologies, evaluation, and potential application of user interfaces aesthetics. Concretely, we aim to: map the state-of-art of aesthetic research in HCI field, build a multidisciplinary community of experts, and raise the profile of this computational research within ISS community.

Call for Participation

Our world is increasingly built up with digital interactive surfaces and spaces in diverse modalities. As one of the most professional academic community of interactive techniques, ISS community keeps working on creating the unique experience on user interfaces. Aesthetics research aims to contribute to further interface experience design as a basic component, which will help designers to evaluate their positions and do practice in aesthetic perspective. Furthermore, the research of aesthetics will shed light on interface optimization. Overall, our workshop will provide the community with an opportunity to enrich the research repository so that discuss future research directions. With continuing the community building, we hope to raise the awareness of computational aesthetics among ISS peers.

Submission and publication

  1. Participants are required to submit a 4-6 page position paper formatted according to the SIGCHI Extended Abstracts publication format.
  2. Papers should be submitted through ISS Precision Conference system
  3. Conference registration will open shortly via the ISS Conference Website. Please note that all participants of the workshop will need to register for at least one day of the full conference.
  4. Extended versions of a selection of high quality papers will be proposed to be submitted to a Special Issue of an International Journal in the field of HCI.
  5. All accepted papers will be published in the companion proceedings published by ACM Digital Library.
